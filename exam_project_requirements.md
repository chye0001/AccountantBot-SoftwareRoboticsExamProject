# Exam Project Requirements

## Exam Project

You must choose your own case and develop a **system or prototype** that automates or supports a meaningful part of a realistic business process.

Your project must show that you can:
- understand and describe a business process
- identify where improvement or automation makes sense
- design and build a relevant technical RPA solution
- reflect on the strengths and limitations of your solution

---

## What You Must Hand In

You must hand in **two deliverables**:

1. **A system or prototype**
2. **A synopsis of maximum 5 pages**

Both deliverables must be based on the same chosen case.

---

## Requirements for Your Chosen Case

Your chosen case must:
- be based on a **realistic and repeatable business process**
- have a **clear start** and a **clear end**
- include at least **one decision point**
- include at least **one exception, deviation, or alternative path**
- involve **data** that is read, validated, transformed, and stored
- involve interaction between **roles, users, and systems**

Choose a case that is large enough to analyse and improve, but still small enough to explain and demonstrate clearly.

---

## Requirements for the System or Prototype

Your system or prototype must:
- support or automate a **meaningful part** of the chosen business process
- reflect an **improved version** of the process, not just isolated technical functionality
- be possible to **demonstrate** during the exam
- handle the **normal process flow**
- handle at least **one exception, error, alternative path**
- make relevant use of **data** in the process

You do **not** need to automate the entire process. A focused solution is acceptable when the scope is clearly chosen and well justified.

---

## Requirements for the Synopsis

The synopsis must be a maximum of **5 pages** and must clearly explain your project.

Your synopsis must include the following:

### 1. Chosen Case and Process
Describe the chosen case and briefly explain the business process.

### 2. Problem and Improvement Potential
Explain why the process is suitable for improvement or automation.
You can, for example, point to repetition, delays, manual work, risk of errors, or lack of consistency.

### 3. AS-IS and TO-BE
Describe:
- the **current process** (AS-IS)
- the **improved process** (TO-BE)

It must be clear what you changed and why.

### 4. System or Prototype
Explain what your system or prototype does, which part of the process it supports, and why you chose that scope.

### 5. Data, Decisions, and Exceptions
Explain:
- what data is used in the process
- what decision point(s) exist in the process
- how your solution handles at least one exception, error, or alternative path

### 6. Value and Limitations
Reflect briefly on:
- what your solution improves
- what your solution does not cover
- what you would improve in a later version

---

## General Expectations

Your project is expected to show:
- process understanding
- relevant improvement thinking
- relevant use of automation
- a clear connection between the process and the technical solution
- reflection on strengths and limitations

---

## What Is Not Required

You are **not** required to:
- automate the entire process end to end
- build an enterprise-ready solution

The focus is on choosing a relevant case, building a demonstrable solution, and explaining your choices clearly.

---

## Summary

Your project must:
- be based on a self-chosen and realistic business process
- include a system or prototype
- show improvement from AS-IS to TO-BE
- include decisions, data, and exception handling
- be documented in a synopsis of maximum 5 pages
